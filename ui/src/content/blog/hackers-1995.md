I watched Hackers for the first time and most of it just felt kinda dull. The movie is loud and flashy, full of bright neon visuals, constant music, and hacking scenes that make no sense. People type frantically while glowing graphics spin around them, and the dialogue is artificial. It’s clear that the makers didn’t understand what they were depicting.

The story isn’t very strong either. It moves quickly but without much weight, and most of the characters feel more like style than substance. I was surprised to see a tomboy Angelina Jolie but I  don't think she had much to work with. The movie seems more focused on looking cool than saying anything meaningful.

What stood out to me, though, was the Hacker Manifesto. The speech about hackers being outcasts who question authority and seek knowledge is surprisingly sincere and resonates with me. It’s short and a little corny, but it gives the film a sense of purpose. For a few minutes, the movie stops being about glowing screens and rollerblades and actually tries to say something about curiosity and rebellion. That part makes the film feel less empty than the rest of it.

I didn’t love it and I probably won’t watch it again, but it isn’t completely without merit. An hour and a half well wasted.

5.5/10
