I watched the _Chainsaw Man: Reze Arc_. I finished it, sat in the dark for a minute, and just felt... a quiet kind of sad. Not the crying kind, but the kind that sits in your chest like a weight.

You know that feeling when you have a really, really good dream? The kind where you're happy, and you're with someone, and everything makes sense? And then you wake up, and for a few seconds it's still real? And then it dissolves, and the real world, the one with your boring room and your responsibilities comes crashing back?

This whole movie is that feeling. That specific, hollow ache.

### It Starts With a Dream He Can't Remember
Denji's having actual dreams about a sealed-up(?) Pochita, and then wakes up into another one.
![Pochita: Never open it!](/blog/chainsaw-man-reze-arc/pochita.jpg)

Makima sees Denji feeling down and decides to cheer him up, by going on a date.
Her idea of a date? Watching ten movies in one day. Why not? She is not busy leading the devil hunter association or anything of that nature.
![Movie date with Makima](/blog/chainsaw-man-reze-arc/movie.jpg)

### Then she’s there. Reze.
![Reze in the cafe](/blog/chainsaw-man-reze-arc/reze-cafe.jpg)

It isn't dramatic. She's just in the cafe, and she's looking _into_ him. She sees the lonely kid.
She asks him about school. He Says he never went. Just another fact of his life, like having a chainsaw for a heart.
And on her face, you don't see pity. You see recognition. _Oh. You missed it, too._ You don't even know what you missed.

So when she leans forward, this girl who seems to speak the language of his loneliness, and whispers her perfectly insane idea... "Let's break into a school"... he doesn't hesitate.
She isn't offering a date. She's offering a shared secret. A chance to rebuild a stolen past, together, in the dark.
![Reze: Let's go to school at night](/blog/chainsaw-man-reze-arc/school-night.jpg)


They sit at desks. They run in the halls. They sneak into the pool at night. The [music](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CSPvJngUn0E) that plays isn't a soundtrack; it's the sound your heart makes when, for the first time in forever, you feel light. You feel normal. You feel like a person, and not a weapon or a debt or a devil.

I held my breath during that pool scene. I knew it couldn't last. Stories like this never let it last. But god, I wanted it to.
![School pool scene](/blog/chainsaw-man-reze-arc/pool.jpg)

After they are done, Reze goes to the toilet to do her business, leaving Denji wrestling with his feelings; wondering if his true love is for Makima or Reze. It was there that we find Denji's standards for an ideal partner:
1. Has to be a woman.
2. Doesn't actively try to kill him.

Pretty high standards, if you ask me. I don't mind my partner trying to kill me.

Going back to Reze, we find her running for her life being chased by an assassin targeting Denji. My heart was pounding, I thought "Here it is. The tragic death to motivate the mc."

But no.

It's Reze, cornered. And her eye... it twitches. It's not fear. It's the pure, incandescent rage of someone being robbed. Someone just took your favorite thing, your one good day, and smashed it right in front of you.
![School pool scene](/blog/chainsaw-man-reze-arc/reze-eye-twitch.jpg)

She kills that assassin, as a furious child saying _You ruined our game!_
![Reze kills assassin](/blog/chainsaw-man-reze-arc/reze-kills-assassin.jpg)

### The Fireworks Festival
She asks him to run away. It's her mission, but in that moment, with the fake sparks in her real eyes, it's also her last, best hope.

And he says no.

He chooses Makima. He chooses the leash he knows over the freedom he can't even imagine.
![Reze finds out about Denji](/blog/chainsaw-man-reze-arc/reze-finds-out.jpg)

And it makes perfect sense. Have you ever stayed in a bad situation because the scary unknown was even scarier? That's Denji. His cage is familiar. Its pain is a known quantity. Love is not.

This part of the movie isn't about battles. It's about the quiet moments in between. The moments where two people, for a split second, build a tiny, beautiful shelter out of "what if."

I don't want to go into other details, I'll just end it here.

8.7/10
